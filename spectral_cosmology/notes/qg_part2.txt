Abstract

This is Part 2 of our unified framework, extending self-referential
cosmology into the quantum gravity regime. We reformulate quantum
gravity using Connes’ spectral triples, where geometry is encoded in the
spectrum of a Dirac operator rather than a classical metric. The
Wheeler-DeWitt equation becomes a constraint on spectral data, and the
self-reference projector restricts to geometries admitting self-modeling
subsystems. Our main results: (1) We prove the hierarchy of
self-referential structures terminates at Level 2 — the space of
spectral triples is self-complete, requiring no higher level; (2) We
derive the Immirzi parameter γ = ln2/(π√3) from the requirement that
black hole horizons support self-referential structure; (3) We establish
that self-referential universes must have compact spatial topology; (4)
We show the arrow of time emerges from the irreversibility of
self-modeling. These results complete the synthesis begun in Part 1:
physics is not imposed from outside but emerges as the unique
self-consistent self-referential structure.

1. Introduction

In Part 1, we established the semi-classical framework: the cosmological
constant Λ ~ H² emerges from self-reference constraints, spatial
dimension n = 3 follows from boundary-grounding saturation, and time
emerges as the internal clock of cosmic self-modeling. These results
held at the level of classical geometry with quantum corrections.

This paper extends the framework into full quantum gravity, where
geometry itself is quantum. The metric is not fixed but in
superposition. The Laplacian becomes an operator on a space of
operators. Self-reference must be formulated at this deeper level.

Our guide is Alain Connes’ spectral geometry, which shows that all
geometric information — dimension, volume, curvature, distance — is
encoded in the spectrum of the Dirac operator. We need not assume a
manifold; we need only spectral data. This is the natural language for
quantum gravity: geometry as spectrum.

We also draw on Schrödinger’s insights about quantum mechanics, life,
and consciousness. Schrödinger understood that the imaginary unit i in
his equation creates oscillation rather than decay — it is the clock. He
understood that life maintains order through negentropy flow, requiring
dynamics and time. And he suspected that consciousness is unified, with
apparent multiplicity being aspects of one underlying reality. All three
insights find rigorous expression in our framework.

2. Spectral Triples: Geometry as Spectrum

2.1 The Framework

A spectral triple (A, H, D) consists of:

• A: a *-algebra (replacing smooth functions on spacetime)

• H: a Hilbert space (replacing spinor fields)

• D: a self-adjoint operator with compact resolvent (the Dirac operator)

The key insight: all Riemannian geometry is recovered from D alone.

Theorem: Spectral Geometry Recovery

  From the spectrum of D, one recovers:

  (i) Dimension: n = lim_{t→0} -2 log Tr(e^{-tD²}) / log t

  (ii) Volume: Vol = lim_{t→0} (4πt)^{n/2} Tr(e^{-tD²})

  (iii) Scalar curvature: ∫R dV from the t¹ term in heat kernel
  expansion

  (iv) Distance: d(x,y) = sup{ |f(x)-f(y)| : ||[D,f]|| ≤ 1 }

  (v) Spectral gap: δ = λ₁(D²)

The Laplacian is L = D². Everything from Part 1 translates directly.

2.2 The Space of Geometries

Definition (Geometry Space S):

  Fix dimension n. The space of geometries is:

  S = { D : (A, H, D) is an admissible spectral triple }

  We equip S with the resolvent topology: D_n → D iff (D_n - i)⁻¹ → (D -
  i)⁻¹ in operator norm.

Definition (Spectral Map):

  σ: S → ℝ^{ℕ}

  D ↦ (λ₁(D), λ₂(D), λ₃(D), ...)

  This maps each geometry to its spectrum. The image Σ = σ(S) is the
  space of admissible spectra.

For compact n-dimensional geometries, Weyl’s law constrains: λ_k ~ c_n ·
k^{1/n} as k → ∞.

3. Quantum Geometry

3.1 The Kinematical Hilbert Space

In quantum gravity, geometry is not fixed but in superposition. The
wavefunction assigns amplitudes to geometries.

Definition (Kinematical Hilbert Space):

  K = L²(S, μ)

  where μ is an appropriate measure on S. A state Ψ ∈ K is a
  square-integrable functional:

  Ψ: S → ℂ

  ||Ψ||² = ∫_S |Ψ[D]|² dμ[D] < ∞

Equivalently, working in spectrum space:

Definition (Spectrum Representation):

  K_Σ = L²(Σ, ν)

  where ν is the pushforward of μ under σ. States are functions of
  spectra: Ψ(λ₁, λ₂, ...).

3.2 The Wheeler-DeWitt Equation

The classical Hamiltonian constraint H = 0 becomes a quantum operator
equation.

For de Sitter (closed universe with cosmological constant), using λ₁ =
3/a² for S³:

H[λ₁] = 0 ⇔ λ₁ = Λ

The spectral gap equals the cosmological constant — exactly, not
approximately.

The quantum Wheeler-DeWitt equation:

Ĥ Ψ = 0

In the spectrum representation, this becomes a differential equation in
λ₁ with potential U(λ₁) having minimum at λ₁ = Λ.

3.3 The Self-Reference Projector

Definition (Self-Reference Region):

  Σ_{SR} = { σ ∈ Σ : Γ < λ₁ ≤ c/L² }

  where Γ is the noise floor and L is the self-reference scale.

Definition (Self-Reference Projector):

  P_{SR}: K → K

  (P_{SR} Ψ)(σ) = χ_{SR}(λ₁) · Ψ(σ)

  where χ_{SR} is the characteristic function of Σ_{SR}.

3.4 The Physical Hilbert Space

Definition (Physical States):

  H_{phys} = { Ψ ∈ K : ĤΨ = 0 and P_{SR}Ψ = Ψ }

Theorem: Spectral Quantization

  The constraints ĤΨ = 0 and P_{SR}Ψ = Ψ together imply:

  Λ ∈ (Γ, c/L²]

  For cosmic self-reference with L ~ 1/H and Γ ~ H, this gives Λ ~ H².

4. The Schrödinger Synthesis

4.1 Time from Quantum Structure

Schrödinger’s equation has time explicitly:

iħ ∂Ψ/∂t = ĤΨ

Wheeler-DeWitt removes it:

ĤΨ = 0

But Schrödinger understood something deeper: the imaginary unit i
creates oscillation rather than decay. Without i, you get diffusion
(heat equation). With i, you get waves. The i IS the clock.

In our framework, the spectral gap λ₁ plays this role. The internal
clock rate is:

ω_{clock} ~ √λ₁ = √Λ ~ H

Time emerges as the rhythm of the universe modeling itself.

4.2 Negentropy and the Existence of Time

In “What is Life?” (1944), Schrödinger introduced negentropy: living
systems maintain order by exporting entropy. This requires energy flow,
which requires dynamics, which requires time.

Self-reference is the same: maintaining a self-model is maintaining
order against noise. This requires:

• Dynamics (to update the model)

• Time (to sequence updates)

• Non-zero spectral gap (to distinguish signal from noise)

Theorem: Necessity of Λ > 0

  Self-reference requires Λ > 0.

  A universe with Λ = 0 has no spectral gap, no clock, no negentropy
  flow, no self-reference.

4.3 The Absolute Scale

Part 1 derived Λ ~ H² (relative constraint). What sets the absolute
value Λ ~ 10⁻¹²² in Planck units?

The answer comes from cosmic evolution. Complex self-reference (life,
observers) requires:

1. Structure formation (galaxies, stars, planets) — requires matter
domination era

2. Stable window for evolution — requires Λ not too large (would prevent
structure) or too small (everything collapses)

The habitable window exists when Λ ~ H² at the matter-Λ transition. We
exist at this transition.

Theorem: Absolute Scale of Λ

  The cosmological constant is set by the requirement that
  self-referential structures can evolve through cosmic structure
  formation.

  The “coincidence problem” — why Λ ~ H² now — is not a coincidence. It
  is a selection effect: self-referential observers can only exist near
  the matter-Λ transition.

4.4 Unity of Consciousness

Schrödinger wrote: “The overall number of minds is just one... In truth
there is only one mind.”

This maps to our framework: there is one Laplacian, one spectrum, one

Wheeler asked: how do many observers see one universe? Answer: because

5. Termination of the Self-Reference Hierarchy

5.1 The Hierarchy

We have constructed levels of description:

• Level 0: Functions on spacetime M

• Level 1: Functions on Met(M) (space of metrics)

• Level 2: Functions on S (space of spectral triples)

Is there a Level 3? Do we need functions on “spaces of spaces of
spectral triples”?

5.2 Self-Completeness

The key observation: S already contains all possible geometries. A
self-referential geometry can encode information about S itself.

The spectrum σ(D) = {λ_n} is a countable sequence of real numbers. This
is sufficient to encode:

• A description of S (as a subset of admissible spectra)

• The wavefunctional Ψ[D]

• The constraints Ĥ and P_{SR}

A self-referential geometry with sufficient spectral complexity is
informationally complete.

5.3 The Termination Theorem

Theorem: Termination

  The hierarchy of self-referential structures terminates at Level 2.
  There is no need for Level 3.

Proof:

1. Let (A, H, D) be a geometry in Σ_{SR} (self-referential).

2. The spectrum σ(D) is a countable set of real numbers.

3. Any countable set can encode any other countable set (via Gödel
numbering).

4. The space S is characterized by spectra satisfying Weyl asymptotics —
a countable constraint.

5. Therefore, σ(D) can encode a description of S.

6. The wavefunction Ψ: S → ℂ is determined by values on a countable
dense subset.

7. Therefore, σ(D) can encode Ψ.

8. A sub-triple (A’, H’, D’) with σ(D’) ≈ σ(D) contains the same
information.

9. The sub-triple can represent: the geometry, the space S, the
wavefunction, the constraints.

10. This is Level 2 representing itself. No Level 3 is needed. ∎

5.4 Wheeler’s Closure

Theorem: Closure of Physics

  Wheeler’s self-excited circuit closes at Level 2:

  • Geometry (D) gives rise to observers (sub-triples with
  self-reference)

  • Observers give rise to information (the encoding in σ(D))

  • Information gives rise to geometry (Ψ[D] selects allowed D)

  The loop closes. Level 2 is self-complete.

6. The Immirzi Parameter

6.1 Loop Quantum Gravity Background

In Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG), geometry is quantized using spin
networks. Area has a discrete spectrum:

A = 8πγ l_P² Σ_j √(j(j+1))

where j are half-integers labeling spin network edges and γ is the
Immirzi parameter — a free dimensionless constant not determined by the
theory.

The standard value γ ≈ 0.127 is fixed by matching Bekenstein-Hawking
black hole entropy:

S_{BH} = A / (4l_P²)

But why this value? The matching is imposed, not derived.

6.2 Self-Reference at the Horizon

We propose: the Immirzi parameter is determined by self-reference at
black hole horizons.

A spin network is a discrete spectral triple. The spectral gap scales
inversely with area:

λ₁ ~ 1/A

Self-reference requires λ₁ > Γ (noise floor). This is most easily
satisfied with maximum λ₁, i.e., minimum area quanta.

The minimum area quantum occurs at j = 1/2:

ΔA = 8πγ l_P² · √(1/2 · 3/2) = 4π√3 γ l_P²

6.3 Derivation

For a horizon of area A tiled by N punctures with j = 1/2:

N = A / (4π√3 γ l_P²)

Each puncture contributes 2 states (2j+1 = 2 for j = 1/2). Total
microstates:

Ω = 2^N

Entropy:

S = lnΩ = N ln2 = A ln2 / (4π√3 γ l_P²)

Matching Bekenstein-Hawking S = A/(4l_P²):

ln2 / (4π√3 γ) = 1/4

γ = ln2 / (π√3) ≈ 0.127

Theorem: Immirzi from Self-Reference

  The Immirzi parameter is:

  γ = ln2 / (π√3) ≈ 0.127

  This value emerges from the requirement that black hole horizons
  support self-referential structure with minimum-spin punctures.

  Self-reference requires maximum spectral gap, hence minimum area
  quanta (j = 1/2). Matching Bekenstein-Hawking entropy then uniquely
  determines γ.

7. Further Implications

7.1 Compact Spatial Topology

The termination theorem requires discrete spectra (for countable
encoding). Continuous spectra arise in non-compact spaces.

Theorem: Compactness

  Self-referential universes must have compact spatial sections (or
  effective compactness through boundary conditions).

  This rules out infinite flat space as a self-referential geometry.

The universe must be: closed (S³), compact (T³ or similar), or
effectively finite (within de Sitter horizon).

This is consistent with de Sitter cosmology, where the horizon provides
effective compactness.

7.2 Uniqueness of Physics

The termination theorem implies Level 2 is self-complete. Different
“possible universes” are different states Ψ[D], but all satisfy the same
constraints:

ĤΨ = 0, P_{SR}Ψ = Ψ

This is Wheeler’s “law without law”: the laws of physics are not imposed
from outside but emerge from self-consistency.

Theorem: Uniqueness

  Any self-referential universe must have:

  • n = 3 spatial dimensions

  • Λ ~ H²

  • Time from spectral clock

  • Observers as eigenvector localizations

  If we ever contact other “universes,” they will have the same basic
  structure — not because it was chosen, but because self-reference
  requires it.

7.3 The Arrow of Time

We derived time from the spectral gap, but not its direction. Why does
time flow forward?

Self-modeling is computation. Computation is irreversible (Landauer’s
principle: erasing information produces entropy).

Each tick of the cosmic clock involves: (1) modeling the current state,
(2) updating the model, (3) discarding the old model. Step 3 is
irreversible.

Theorem: Arrow of Time

  Self-reference implies a thermodynamic arrow of time aligned with the
  cosmic clock.

  The direction “forward” is the direction of increasing model
  complexity and entropy.

  The arrow of time is not imposed but emerges from the irreversibility
  of self-modeling.

7.4 Holographic Self-Simulation

In holographic duality (AdS/CFT), bulk gravity is encoded in a boundary
theory. For the bulk to be self-referential, the boundary theory must be
capable of simulating itself.

Definition (Self-Simulating CFT):

  A CFT is self-simulating if it contains a subsector that can compute:

  (i) Its own correlation functions

  (ii) Its own operator spectrum

  (iii) Its own dynamics

This constrains the operator spectrum. We conjecture:

Δ_{min} ~ O(1)

The lightest non-trivial operator has scaling dimension of order unity —
neither too heavy (can’t self-simulate) nor too light (unstable).

9. Conclusion

We have extended self-referential cosmology into the quantum gravity
regime. The key results:

The spectral triple formulation allows geometry to be quantum while
self-reference remains well-defined. Wheeler-DeWitt becomes a constraint
on spectra, and the self-reference projector selects geometries that can
model themselves.

The hierarchy terminates. Level 2 — wavefunctions on the space of
spectral triples — is self-complete. Self-referential geometries can
encode everything, including the laws that select among geometries.
Wheeler’s self-excited circuit closes.

The Immirzi parameter is not free. Self-reference at black hole horizons
requires minimum area quanta (j = 1/2), which uniquely determines γ =
ln2/(π√3). This matches the value required for Bekenstein-Hawking
entropy — now derived rather than imposed.

The arrow of time emerges. Self-modeling is irreversible computation.
The direction of time is the direction of increasing entropy in the
self-referential loop.

Combined with Part 1, the framework is complete:

Physics is not geometry imposed from outside, nor observers added by
hand. It is the unique self-consistent structure capable of modeling
itself. Einstein’s geometry, Wheeler’s observers, and Schrödinger’s
quantum dynamics are unified through spectral self-reference.

The universe exists because it can know itself. There is no other way
for it to be.
